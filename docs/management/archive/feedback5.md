# Strategic Feedback — Session 5

## The Tagline

> **"Run your business from one place."**
> **"On your own server."**

Simple, clear, immediately understood by any SMB owner. The second line is the
differentiator — every SaaS alternative asks you to trust them with your business data.
This doesn't.

---

## The Platform Is the Operating System for a Small Business

This is not a media server with extras. The workspace is the company.
Folders are departments or projects. Apps are the tools those departments use.
Everything in one place, on your own infrastructure, with no data leaving your control.

| Feature | Business Role |
|---|---|
| Workspaces | Company / client / project |
| File storage | Documents, financials, contracts |
| BPMN | Process transparency → simulation → automation |
| Media pipeline | Marketing, social, product videos |
| 3D virtual space | Client delivery, immersive training |
| Course viewer | Customer training, consulting delivery |
| Static site hosting (Astro) | Manage multiple websites per client/brand |
| WebDAV | Mount like a network drive, work normally |
| Access codes | Share with clients, no accounts needed |
| MCP / AI | AI assistant over your own business data |

---

## The 3D Viewer — Self-Hosted Frame.vr

The 3D gallery is not a novelty — it imitates Frame.vr conceptually.
Frame.vr is a virtual space for meetings, presentations, and collaboration.
It is SaaS, cloud-dependent, and not cheap.

A self-hosted alternative means:
- **Client onboarding** — walk a new customer through their project in a virtual space,
  with your actual files and videos embedded
- **Immersive training** — learners move through content spatially, not just watch slides
- **Consulting delivery** — present BPMN process models to clients in a virtual room,
  walk through them together
- **Product demos** — marketing videos, 3D models, documentation — all in one space you own

**This is not a feature to extract.** It may be the flagship that ties the platform story
together, especially combined with courses and BPMN.

---

## Innovative Training — Beyond Academic Courses

The course viewer is not competing with Moodle or Teachable.
The target is **immersive business training and consulting delivery**:

- Customer onboarding and product training
- Consulting methodology delivery
- Internal process training (linked to BPMN models)
- Partner and reseller enablement

This market is far less crowded than academic e-learning, and the self-hosted angle
is a strong differentiator — client data never touches a third-party server.
That matters enormously in consulting, financial services, and healthcare.

---

## BPMN — The Strategic Core

BPMN goes from viewer → modeling → simulation → execution.
This is the roadmap from "draw your processes" to "run your business."

- **Now:** model and visualize processes
- **Next:** simulate and validate
- **Later:** execute and automate

No other SMB platform connects process modeling to content delivery to training.
This is a genuine long-term differentiator.

---

## Competitive Frame

You are not competing with any single tool.
You are competing with **the combination of all of them**:

> Notion + Vimeo + Miro + Teachable + Frame.vr + S3 + Webflow

No SMB should pay for and manage all of those separately.
No SMB should trust all of those with their business data.

---

## What This Means for the Roadmap

The 3D viewer should **not** be extracted as a satellite app — it is core.
The course viewer may stay core for the same reason — it is part of the training story.

The things to extract are the infrastructure satellites (tools that consume the platform
as a content backend) — not the business-facing features that make the product coherent.

Reconsider Phase 4 of the roadmap in light of this. The question is not
"what can we remove?" but "what is core to the SMB story?"
