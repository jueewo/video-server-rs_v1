# Branding Notes

## Current Name: AppKask

- Short, techy, suggests "app" + "cask" (container/vault)
- Maps to workspace/vault architecture
- Signals tech/platform clearly
- Feels developer-oriented — less ideal for business/consultant audience

## Candidate: Mustcato (mustcato.com)

### Full Acronym: MUSTCATO

> **My U**nified **S**ystems, **T**eams, **C**ontent, **A**gents, **T**ools, **O**perations

| Letter | Word | Platform mapping |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| **M** | My | Personal ownership — your server, your data, your platform |
| **U** | Unified | Single platform, not seven tools |
| **S** | Systems | Workspaces, folders, self-hosted infrastructure |
| **T** | Teams | Users, groups, access control, sharing |
| **C** | Content | Media, documents, courses, sites |
| **A** | Agents | AI agents with roles, hierarchy, workflows |
| **T** | Tools | BPMN, 3D spaces, presentations, apps |
| **O** | Operations | Orchestration, federation, delivery tiers |

### M/U variations considered

| Combo | Reads as | Feel |
|-------|----------|------|
| **My** Unified | *My* unified S,T,C,A,T,O | Warm, personal — reinforces self-hosted ownership. **Chosen.** |
| Manage Unified | Manage your unified S,T,C,A,T,O | Professional, functional — original version |
| Master Unified | Master your unified S,T,C,A,T,O | Confident, premium — slightly aggressive |
| Mobilize Unified | Mobilize your unified S,T,C,A,T,O | Active, dynamic — more enterprise-y |

"My" was chosen because it reinforces the self-hosted "yours" narrative and matches the moscato warmth.

### Pros
- Warm, approachable, premium feel (moscato/artisan association)
- Strong acronym that doubles as elevator pitch
- Memorable and globally pronounceable
- Fits business/consultant/researcher audience
- Very googleable, domain available

### Cons
- No tech signal in the name (tagline must carry that)
- "Must" prefix sounds a bit hard/demanding — could soften perception

### Pronunciation

**moos-KAH-toh** (Italian style, like moscato)

This is the official pronunciation. Avoids the hard English "must" connotation entirely. Reads warm and premium. Natural for European markets; English speakers pick it up immediately once told.

### Note on "Must" sounding hard

The English word "must" carries an obligation/demanding tone. Mitigations:
- The Italian-style pronunciation (moos-KAH-toh) eliminates this entirely
- The written form reads softer than spoken English "MUST-cah-toh"
- In most European languages "must" doesn't carry the obligation connotation
- Include pronunciation guide in brand materials and onboarding

### Recommendation

Use **Mustcato** for the business-facing brand. The acronym is the differentiator — use it subtly in marketing ("it happens to stand for..."), let the name breathe as a brand on its own.

**AppKask** can remain as the internal/technical project name or open-source identity if those paths ever diverge.

## Also Considered: Aipokit (aipokit.com)

Reads as "AI pocket" — everything in one place, self-contained.

| | Mustcato | Aipokit |
|---|---|---|
| Signals AI | No (subtle) | Yes, front and center |
| Signals self-contained | No | Yes ("pocket") |
| Acronym potential | Strong (MUSTCATO) | Weak |
| Premium/warm feel | Yes | Techy/playful |
| Timeless | Yes | Risky — "AI" in names may age as AI becomes ambient |
| Business audience | Fits well | Feels startup-y |
| Consultant delivery | "Your Mustcato is ready" — gravitas | "Your Aipokit is ready" — lighter |
| Pronunciation | Clear once set | Clear immediately |

**Decision**: Not chosen. Putting "AI" in the brand name is a strong bet on the current moment. In 2-3 years when AI is everywhere, it risks feeling dated (like "InternetCo" in 2005). The platform does much more than AI — media, courses, BPMN, sites, federation — and agents are one piece. Better to signal AI in the tagline than bake it into the name.

## Target Audience (for branding decisions)

Primary: business owners, consultants, researchers
Secondary: regulated industries, knowledge-based businesses
Tertiary: developers (they'll find it anyway)

## Tagline (current)
